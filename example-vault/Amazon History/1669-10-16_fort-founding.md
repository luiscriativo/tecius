On October 16, 1669, Portuguese captain **Francisco da Mota Falcão** ordered the construction of **Fort São José da Barra do Rio Negro** at the confluence of the Rio Negro and the Amazon River. This modest military outpost — built to assert Portuguese dominance over the region and repel Spanish and Dutch incursions — would grow over the next two centuries into the city of **Manaus**.

The location was chosen strategically: the dark waters of the Rio Negro meeting the muddy Amazon created a natural landmark. Indigenous Manaós people had long inhabited these banks, and their name would eventually give the city its identity.

## From Fort to City

- **1695** — Settlement expands around the fort; first permanent civilian inhabitants
- **1755** — Town of Lugar da Barra do Rio Negro officially founded
- **1832** — Elevated to the status of Vila
- **1848** — Recognized as a city; named *Manaus* after the indigenous Manaós people
- **1850** — Becomes capital of the newly created Province of Amazonas
