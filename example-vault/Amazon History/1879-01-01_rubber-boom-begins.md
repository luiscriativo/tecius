By the late 1870s, the global demand for natural rubber — driven by the industrial revolution and, later, the bicycle and automobile industries — had ignited an economic explosion in the Amazon basin. The region held a near-monopoly on *Hevea brasiliensis*, the rubber tree that produced the world's finest latex.

Manaus became the **commercial heart of the rubber world**. Fortunes were made in months. River barons grew obscenely wealthy. The city transformed almost overnight from a provincial backwater into a hub of international commerce.

## The Rubber Economy

- At its peak, Amazonas produced over **40% of the world's rubber supply**
- Rubber accounted for more than **25% of Brazil's total export revenue**
- Thousands of *seringueiros* (rubber tappers) — many of them migrants from Brazil's drought-stricken northeast — worked in near-slavery conditions deep in the forest
- European luxury goods flooded Manaus: French wine, Italian marble, Belgian crystal
- Wealthy rubber barons sent their laundry to Lisbon and their children to schools in Paris

> "The streets of Manaus ran not with mud, but with money."
