On February 28, 1967, the Brazilian military government created the **Zona Franca de Manaus** (Manaus Free Trade Zone) by Decree-Law No. 288. The policy granted sweeping tax incentives to companies that established manufacturing operations in Manaus — a deliberate strategy to occupy and develop the Amazon region, and to provide an economic engine for a city that had been economically stagnant for half a century.

The logic was geopolitical as much as economic: a populated, industrialized Manaus would discourage foreign encroachment into the Amazon basin.

## Impact

- Manaus's population grew from **300,000 in 1967** to over **2 million by 2010**
- The city became one of Brazil's largest electronics manufacturing hubs
- Companies including **Nokia, Samsung, Philips, Honda**, and dozens of others established plants
- The Industrial District (*Distrito Industrial*) employs hundreds of thousands of workers
- Manaus became the **fourth-largest city in Brazil** by the early 21st century

The Free Trade Zone remains active today, generating over $20 billion USD in industrial output annually, and is considered one of the most successful regional development experiments in Brazilian history.
