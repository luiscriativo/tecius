By imperial decree on September 5, 1850, Emperor **Dom Pedro II** signed Law No. 582, separating the upper Amazon region from the Province of Grão-Pará and creating the **Province of Amazonas** — the largest province in the Brazilian Empire by territory.

Manaus was designated its capital. At the time, the entire province had fewer than **50,000 inhabitants** scattered across a territory larger than Western Europe. Roads were nonexistent; the rivers were the only highways.

The creation of the province was both a political and strategic act: Brazil needed to assert sovereignty over the vast Amazon basin, increasingly coveted by European powers and neighboring nations.

> The province encompassed over 1.5 million square kilometers — a territory larger than France, Spain, Portugal, and Germany combined.

## First Years of the Province

- **João Batista de Figueiredo Tenreiro Aranha** appointed first president of the province
- Infrastructure was almost entirely absent — governance operated through river travel
- Indigenous population still represented the majority of inhabitants in the interior
- The rubber tree (*Hevea brasiliensis*) was already being tapped, but the great boom was still decades away
