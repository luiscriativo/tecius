The original **Fort São José da Barra do Rio Negro** was a simple wooden palisade erected at the confluence of the Rio Negro and the Amazon. It was modest by any measure — a rectangular enclosure with a small garrison — but it was the first permanent European structure in the region and the embryo from which Manaus would grow.

The fort was rebuilt in stone in the 18th century. Though largely gone today, its location on what is now the *Porto de Manaus* (Port of Manaus) remains the historical zero point of the city. A commemorative marker at the port acknowledges the founding date.

## What Remains

The original structure no longer exists in its colonial form. The site was transformed over centuries of port development. However, the **Igreja de São Sebastião** (1888) and surrounding streets in the historic center preserve the general layout of the original settlement that grew around the fort.
