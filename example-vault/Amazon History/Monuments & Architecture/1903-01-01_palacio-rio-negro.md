Built at the beginning of the 20th century as the private residence of German rubber baron **Waldemar Scholz**, the **Palácio Rio Negro** is one of the finest surviving examples of rubber-boom residential architecture in Manaus. Scholz arrived in the Amazon to profit from the rubber trade and built himself a palace that would not have looked out of place in Hamburg or Vienna.

After Scholz's fortune collapsed with the rubber bust, the building was acquired by the state government of Amazonas in 1918 and served as the **official residence and seat of government** for successive governors until 2000.

## The Building

- **Style:** Eclectic — mixing neoclassical and Art Nouveau elements
- **Materials:** European marble, tropical hardwood interiors, imported tiles
- **Grounds:** Formal garden with European-style hedging and fountains
- **Period furnishings:** Much of the original furniture and decor remains intact

Today the Palácio Rio Negro operates as a **public cultural center and museum**, one of the best places in Manaus to experience the material culture of the Belle Époque period. Its ballrooms, libraries, and formal reception rooms are open to visitors, preserved almost exactly as they were at the height of the rubber boom.
